INSPIRATION
IMPRESSIONISM
Impressionism is one of my favorite western art movements. The focus on light more broadly rather than specific details really speaks to me and how I paint. I love the motion that these artists capture with intentional brush strokes and bold colors to portray changing light. Many leaders in impressionism were French, and museums such as the Musée d'Orsay have extensive impressionist collections.
Pictured: Edgar Degas, The Green Dancer
CLAUDE MONET
Villas at Bordighera, 1884
This painting caught my eye because of the color palette. From a distance, it looks very simple and muted, but as you get closer, you can really see the variety of colors in the details and well as the texture of the brush strokes.
LIFESTYLE
While staying in France, we tried to adopt some of the parisian lifestyle into ours. Every morning, we walked from our apartment to the local bakery to pick up the most delicious fresh-baked bread and croissants. For dinner, we would sit and socialize for hours on the covered outdoor patios of bistros. Although most of what we visited was just “tourist” stuff, the ideals of the french lifestyle felt very inspiring.
A bike parked on a bridge over the Seine. I snapped this shot as a glimpse into parisian life. Bikes provide ideal transportation for European city dwellers. This picture has even inspired some paintings already.

INSPIRATION
ANTONI GAUDI
Gaudi is the catalan modernist architect known for his incredible works in Barcelona. He creates intricate and unique buildings inspired by the shapes and forms created by nature. Exploring and experiencing Gaudi’s architecture has really helped me think about how to incorporate more nature-inspired elements into my art in new ways.
Pictured: Guardians, on the rooftop of Casa Mila
Inside Casa Mila, a private residence designed by Gaudi. The building is designed as a constant curve, and shows great examples of Gaudi’s emphasis on natural forms. At first controversial, the rooftop guardians are now a postcard symbol of Barcelona.
The Sagrada Familia, the most famous of Gaudi’s works, pictured with construction. Gaudi heavily incorporated biblical themes with nature, designing the interior of the church to feel like a modern forest full of trees. The church is planned to be completed in 2030.
A mosaic lizard from Park Guell, one of the more famous statues found in the park. This park incorporates nature not only in the architecture, but also in lush and expansive gardens.
SURREALISM
Surrealism is my favorite western art movement. The emphasis on dream-like and absurd objects to create illogical and unnerving scenes fascinates me. We visited Figures, the home town of Salvador Dali and home to his self-curated museum, which was my favorite museum we visited. This museum is surrealism at its finest - it is confusing, nonsensical, and every inch from floor to ceiling is intentional art.
Pictured: Dali's The Daughter of the West Wind, painted on a ceiling
SALVADOR DALI
Mythology: Argus
This is a sketch of Dali’s, he has many of them displayed in the hallways of his museum. This one caught my eye because of all the eyes - Dali uses eyes a lot in his works, and I find them very interesting subjects. In this, he uses them on the tail of a peacock, which puts the eyes in interesting positions.
